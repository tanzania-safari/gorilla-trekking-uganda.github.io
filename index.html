What is Gorilla Trekking Uganda?

Gorilla Trekking Uganda is a once-in-a-lifetime wildlife experience that allows visitors to track endangered mountain gorillas in their natural rainforest habitat. Uganda is home to more than half of the world’s remaining mountain gorillas, making it the leading destination for gorilla trekking globally.

The experience takes place primarily in Bwindi Impenetrable National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its dense rainforest, mist-covered hills, and exceptional biodiversity. Gorilla trekking involves hiking through forest terrain with experienced rangers until you locate a habituated gorilla family.

Once found, visitors spend one unforgettable hour observing gorillas as they feed, groom, and interact naturally.

Where Does Gorilla Trekking in Uganda Take Place?

Gorilla Trekking Uganda happens in Bwindi Impenetrable National Park and Mgahinga Gorilla National Park. However, Bwindi is the primary location and is divided into four main trekking sectors:

Rushaga Sector

Rushaga has the highest number of habituated gorilla families. It is ideal for travelers seeking availability and flexibility.

Nakuringo Sector

Nkuringo offers dramatic scenery and a more physically demanding trek with rewarding panoramic views.

Buhoma Sector

Buhoma is the oldest trekking sector and offers excellent accessibility and lodge options.

Ruhija Sector

Ruhija provides a quieter trekking experience with fewer crowds.

How Much Does Gorilla Trekking Uganda Cost?

A gorilla trekking permit in Uganda costs $800 per person (subject to change by Uganda Wildlife Authority). This permit allows one hour with a habituated gorilla family.

Best Time for Gorilla Trekking Uganda

Gorilla trekking in Uganda is available year-round. However, the best months are during the dry seasons:

  • June to September
  • December to February

These months offer easier hiking conditions and clearer photography opportunities.

Gorilla Trekking Uganda vs Gorilla Tracking Rwanda

While Gorilla Trekking Uganda offers lower permit costs and more gorilla families, gorilla tracking Rwanda provides easier hiking terrain and luxury lodge proximity.
